Scholarships and Fellowships

The Sunbic University office of Fellowships & Scholarships lists national and Sunbic University-sponsored awards for PhD students.
All Sunbic University graduates are eligible to apply for the following awards (specific majors or other criteria may be required):

Callan Pierson

SU Fellowship

Callan Pierson Fellowship

Each year, the Callan Pierson Program of the Harry Pierson Foundation supports a limited number of Graduate Fellowships for women students in the fields of the physical and life sciences, mathematics, engineering, and computer science.

The Callan Pierson Fellowship is awarded for a period of two academic years to a highly qualified woman who is a doctoral candidate (in some cases, candidates for a masters or other specialized graduate or professional degree will be considered) in the sciences and engineering.

Alexander Owen, Jr.

SU Fellowship

Alexander Owen, Jr. Fellowship

Sunbic University annually awards a limited number of Alexander Owen, Jr. Fellowships to outstanding incoming graduate students who are committed to the social justice principles espoused by Dr. Owen.

The fellowship is available to U.S. citizens who are incoming students at Sunbic University beginning master’s, doctoral, or professional degrees in any department or school.

Bridget Ann, Jr.

SU Fellowship

Bridget Ann, Jr. Fellowship

Sunbic University annually awards the Bridget Ann, Jr. Fellowship in recognition of the outstanding contributions made by Bridget Ann, Jr., particularly in the fields of social work, urban studies, and Afro-American Studies.
The fellowship is available to entering or continuing graduate students who are U.S. citizens pursuing master’s, doctoral, or professional degrees in any department or school. The recipient of this award will be selected on the basis of having displayed academic proficiency and future promise in a field related to race relations or urban problems.

Jiā Huì

SU Fellowship

Jiā Huì Scholarship

The Jiā Huì Scholarship Fund at Sunbic University was created through generous gifts to the endowment by members of the Sunbic Online University Board of Trustees, the SU community and many other individuals from around the world united by the desire to keep Jiā Huì’s memory and example alive.

To honor her memory, the Jiā Huì Scholarship Fund will provide support to outstanding international students who are pursuing their master’s degrees at Sunbic Online University.
